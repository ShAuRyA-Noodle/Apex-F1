The modern Formula 1 power unit is a marvel of thermal efficiency, extracting over 50% of the energy from its fuel. But raw power is only half the equation; the delicate interplay between the internal combustion engine (ICE) and the energy recovery systems (ERS) dictates performance in the modern era.

MGU-H: The Silent Workhorse

Sitting nestled in the 'V' of the engine block, the Motor Generator Unit - Heat (MGU-H) is arguably the most complex component. It harvests energy from the exhaust gases, spinning at upwards of 100,000 RPM. This energy is then either stored in the Energy Store (battery) or deployed directly to the MGU-K to aid acceleration.
